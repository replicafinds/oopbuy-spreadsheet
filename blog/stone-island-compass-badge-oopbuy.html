Stone Island Compass Badge Guide — What to Check on OopBuy

Stone Island's embroidered compass badge is the most scrutinized detail in rep outerwear. Getting it right requires a factory with the correct embroidery specifications — and not all sellers use them. Here is how to verify before ordering.

Badge Dimensions

The compass badge has specific dimensions that should be consistent across colorways. Too large or too small is immediately noticeable to anyone familiar with the brand. The badge on a hoodie should sit roughly in the center of the left sleeve section, neither too close to the seam nor too far.

Embroidery Quality

Zoom into QC photos of the badge. The compass face should show clear, defined needle points — the directional markers should be crisp, not blurry. The thread on the outer ring should be smooth and consistent. Loose threads or uneven tension indicate a factory that has not invested in the correct embroidery setup.

The background of the badge is typically white. On quality pieces, the white is dense and clean. On lower-quality pieces it can look thin or slightly grey.

Color Accuracy

The red diamond compass symbol in the center should be a specific shade of red — not too orange and not too dark. The surrounding black or navy border should be consistent and not bleeding into the white field.

For Ghost Piece hoodies (printed rather than embroidered badge), the mesh overlay should sit evenly over a crisp printed design. Bubbling or uneven mesh indicates a quality issue.

How to Request the Right QC Photos

When messaging the seller through OopBuy to request QC photos, specifically ask for: a close-up of the badge face, a side angle showing the badge edges, and a shot showing how the badge attaches to the sleeve. These three photos reveal everything about badge quality.
