The Nike Dunk Low is the most popular rep sneaker in the market right now, which means the seller landscape is highly developed but also crowded with low-quality listings. Here is exactly what to check before ordering.

The Sole Unit

Dunk sole quality is visible from two angles: side profile and bottom. From the side, the sole should show the correct curvature — a slight curve that matches the natural shape of the foot. From the bottom, the outsole pattern should be clearly defined, not shallow or mushy-looking.

The heel area is particularly telling. On quality batches the heel swoosh cutout is clean and precise. On lower-quality batches the edges of this detail are slightly rough or inconsistently shaped.

Toe Box Shape

The Dunk Low toe box has a characteristic shape — slightly rounded, with a defined edge where the toecap meets the rest of the upper. Sellers cutting corners on tooling produce a toe box that is either too pointy or too squared-off. This is one of the first things to check in a QC photo.

Swoosh Placement

The Nike swoosh sits at a specific height on the Dunk — the tail of the swoosh should align with a consistent point on the lateral side. Sellers who have the correct tooling get this right consistently. Sellers who do not produce swooshes that sit slightly too high or too low.
